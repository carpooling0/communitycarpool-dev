Photography & Imagery Overview

Guidelines for selecting, capturing, and using imagery that aligns with Community Carpool's brand identity.

Photography Principles

Community Carpool imagery should feel authentic, inclusive, and human-centered. We avoid stock photos that feel overly staged or generic.

Principle Definition Examples
Authentic Real people, real moments, real emotions Candid commute photos, genuine user interactions
Inclusive Diverse ages, backgrounds, abilities, and lifestyles Varied demographics, different car types, different scenarios
Optimistic Positive emotions, forward-facing, hopeful Smiling faces, helpful moments, successful connections
Environmental Green spaces, sustainable transport, nature Trees, cycling, walking, clean air, eco-friendly imagery
Community-focused People connecting, collaboration, togetherness Group dynamics, shared journeys, neighbor interaction

Photography to Avoid

Don't Use

✗ Overly staged stock photos
✗ Generic business people shaking hands
✗ Unrealistic, too-perfect scenarios
✗ Luxury cars or premium vehicles
✗ Outdated or heavily filtered images
✗ Single demographic (all one race/age/gender)
✗ Unsafe driving scenarios
✗ Polluted or negative urban scenes

Do Use

✓ Authentic, real-life moments
✓ Genuine smiles and natural emotions
✓ Diverse people of all ages/backgrounds
✓ Everyday, relatable vehicles
✓ Minimal filtering, natural colors
✓ Inclusive representation
✓ Safe, responsible driving contexts
✓ Green spaces and nature

Image Usage Guidelines

Web Optimization

✓ Image Optimization

Always optimize images for web: compress files, use appropriate formats, provide multiple resolutions for responsive design, use WebP with JPEG fallback.

✓ Alt Text Requirements

Every image must have descriptive alt text. Format: "[Image description that conveys meaning]". Examples: "Sarah smiling during her morning commute" not "IMG_1234.jpg"

✓ Image Spacing

Leave adequate white space around images. Use 24px+ margin on desktop, 16px+ on mobile. Images should not touch container edges.

Accessibility for Images

Type Alt Text Required? Caption Needed? Example
Decorative No (alt="") No Background patterns, dividers
Informational Yes Optional Photos of people, vehicles
Complex Yes (detailed) Yes Charts, maps, diagrams
Functional Yes (action) Optional Icon buttons, linked images

Copyright & Licensing

✓ Rights Management

All images must have clear licensing. Recommended sources: Unsplash, Pexels (free CC0), or licensed stock photos. Always include proper attribution when required.

✓ User-Generated Content

When featuring user photos or testimonials, obtain explicit permission. Include release forms for identifiable people. Protect privacy at all times.

Photography Scenarios

Hero/Header Images

Purpose: Set emotional tone, build interest, drive conversions
Content: Real people, open roads, nature, optimistic moments
Size: 1920×1080px (16:9) hero-size, 2x for retina
Treatment: Bright, clear, minimal text overlay

Profile/Testimonial Images

Purpose: Build trust, humanize brand, show real users
Content: Genuine smiling faces, diverse demographics
Size: 400×400px minimum (1:1 square crop-friendly)
Treatment: Professional portrait style, good lighting, clear face

Journey Documentation

Purpose: Show real commute experiences
Content: Candid car moments, route scenery, real roads
Size: 1200×900px (4:3) standard, 1080×1080px social
Treatment: Authentic, natural lighting, minimal staging

Feature/Blog Images

Purpose: Support content, break up text
Content: Urban scenes, environmental context, detail shots
Size: 1200×900px (4:3) primary, 600×400px for cards
Treatment: Focused subject, clear composition, relevant to content

Image Editing Guidelines

Editing Standards

Edit Type Acceptable Not Acceptable
Exposure Subtle brightening, shadow recovery Extreme overexposure, blown highlights
Color Natural color correction, white balance Unrealistic color grading, heavy saturation
Cropping Composition improvement, aspect ratio Removing people, changing meaning
Filters Subtle vignette, minimal presets Heavy filters, Instagram-style effects
Retouching Blemish removal, dust cleanup Heavy smoothing, unrealistic faces
Composition Straightening horizons, removing distractions Adding/removing main subjects, manipulating
⚠ Editing Philosophy

Less is more. Images should feel natural and authentic, not heavily edited. Editing should enhance, not transform. If an image needs extensive editing, consider re-shooting instead.
