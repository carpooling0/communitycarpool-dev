🔗 Your Matches

Understanding match strength

Each match is assigned a percentage score that indicates how closely your commute route overlaps with your match's route. A higher percentage means a more convenient carpool.

What the percentage means

The match strength score (e.g. 87% match) is calculated from two factors:

  • Pickup proximity — How close your home area is to your match's home area.
  • Drop-off proximity — How close your workplace is to your match's workplace.

Both are combined into a single score. A 100% score would mean your pickup and drop-off points are at exactly the same locations — which would essentially mean the same address.

Score guide

ScoreWhat it meansRating
90–100%Routes are almost identical. Very little detour needed for either party.Excellent
75–89%Very close routes. A small detour of 1–3 km for pickup or drop-off.Good
60–74%Reasonably close. May require a slightly longer detour.Fair
Below 60%Routes overlap but the detour may be significant. Use your judgement.Low
💡
Don't dismiss lower scores automatically — A 65% match might still be very convenient if, for example, your workplaces are in the same building and only your home areas differ slightly. Look at the route details, not just the number.

What match strength does not measure

The score is purely geographical. It does not account for:

  • Timing — whether you leave at the same time of day.
  • Frequency — whether you both commute on the same days.
  • Driving preference — who will drive.

These are things you can discuss with your match directly once contact details are revealed after a mutual match.
