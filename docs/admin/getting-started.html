Login & navigation

How to access the admin panel, navigate between panels, understand your role, and manage your session.

Accessing the admin panel

The admin panel is a separate login-protected page:

  • Development: https://dev.communitycarpool.org/admin.html
  • Production: https://communitycarpool.org/admin.html

You need an email address and password issued by a Super-Admin. These are separate credentials from any user account you may have on the platform.

To sign in: enter your email and password, then click Sign In.

Navigating panels

The admin panel has a two-part sidebar on the left:

  • Icon strip (48 px wide) — always visible, shows the CCP logo at the top and a Log Out button at the bottom
  • Nav panel (200 px) — shows panel names grouped by section (Overview, Support, Admin, System)

Click any nav item to switch to that panel — the main area updates without a page reload. Click the chevron (‹) button at the top-right of the nav panel to collapse it to icon-only view, giving you more space. Click it again to expand.

Your name and role appear at the bottom of the sidebar, above the Log Out button.

Session & logout

  • Sessions last 8 hours from the time you sign in
  • After 8 hours you are automatically signed out — you will see the login screen on your next action
  • Always click Log Out (bottom of the sidebar) when you are done, especially on a shared or work computer
  • If you are signed out unexpectedly before 8 hours, try logging in again — your session may have been invalidated (for example, if a Super-Admin reset your account). If this keeps happening, notify a Super-Admin.
💡
If you leave the admin panel open in a background tab for several hours and then switch back to it, you may find yourself already signed out. This is normal — just sign in again. Your work is not lost because all actions are written to the database immediately when you take them.

Forgotten password / account issues

  • There is no self-service password reset — contact a Super-Admin to reset your password
  • Your password is hashed in the database and cannot be recovered by anyone — only reset
  • If you suspect your account credentials have been compromised (someone else may have your password), notify a Super-Admin immediately so they can deactivate your account and create a new one with fresh credentials
  • Never share your login credentials with anyone, including colleagues — each team member must have their own account
