🗑️ Operations

Deletion requests

When a user requests account deletion from their matches page, a deletion request is created. You can cancel the request (reverting the user's account to active) or process it immediately using your 6-digit PIN.

ℹ️
This panel is available to Admin and Super-Admin roles only. If you have a Support role and need access, contact a Super-Admin.

How deletion requests are created

The deletion flow is fully automated and user-initiated:

  1. A user clicks "Delete my account" on their matches page (matches.html)
  2. They receive an email with a one-time confirmation link
  3. After clicking the confirmation link, their account enters deletion_pending state
  4. A scheduled deletion date is set: the confirmation date plus the configured data_retention_days (currently 30 days)
  5. Their active journey submissions are paused during this window — they will not appear in new matches
  6. The automated cron job (process-deletions) runs daily at 22:00 UTC (02:00 Dubai time) and permanently deletes accounts whose scheduled deletion date has passed

All accounts in this pending state appear in the Deletions panel until they are either cancelled or processed.

The Deletions panel

The panel shows a table of all accounts currently pending deletion. If no deletions are pending, a checkmark and "No pending deletion requests." is displayed — this is the ideal state.

Cancelling a deletion request

When to use this: a user contacts you saying they clicked "Delete my account" by mistake, or they changed their mind and want their account restored.

1

Verify the user's identity

Confirm that the person requesting the cancellation is the account holder. The most reliable method is to confirm they are emailing you from the same address shown in the Deletions panel. Do not cancel based on a message sent from a different email address without further verification.

2

Find the user in the Deletions panel

Locate their row. Confirm the name and email match the person who contacted you.

3

Click Cancel

Click the Cancel button on their row. No PIN is required for cancellation. A confirmation prompt will appear — confirm to proceed.

4

Account is restored immediately

The account reverts to full active status. Their journeys go back to active, their deletion token is cleared, and they will start appearing in new matches again. Inform the user that their account is active again.

What happens in the system when you cancel

  • deletion_requested_at and deletion_token are cleared from the users record
  • Their submission statuses revert from paused back to active
  • Their match records return to notified status
  • The cancellation is logged in the admin audit log

Deleting immediately (Delete Now)

When to use this: there is an urgent reason to delete the account before the scheduled date. Valid reasons include a confirmed harassment case requiring immediate removal, a legal or GDPR request, or a direct user request for immediate deletion that cannot wait 30 days.

⚠️
Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. There is no recycle bin, no recovery path, and no way to restore the data after deletion. If you are unsure whether to delete or cancel, cancel first and investigate. You can always delete later; you cannot undo a deletion.
1

Click Delete Now

Click the Delete Now button on the user's row.

2

Enter your deletion PIN

A confirmation modal appears with a warning and a 6-digit PIN field. Enter your deletion PIN — this is a separate credential from your login password. You received it when your admin account was created. If you have forgotten it, contact a Super-Admin to reset it.

3

Confirm you have the right user

Before clicking the final confirm button, check the name and email in the modal one more time. This is your last opportunity to verify you are deleting the correct account.

4

Click Delete Permanently

Click Delete Permanently. The deletion executes immediately. The row disappears from the Deletions panel.

What gets deleted

Deletions occur in a specific order to respect foreign key relationships:

  1. All events linked to the user's submissions
  2. All matches involving any of the user's submissions
  3. All submissions belonging to the user
  4. The users record itself

A deletion_log entry is created as a permanent record of the deletion (email hash, date, and who processed it). This log entry is retained even after the user record is gone.

Empty state

When there are no pending deletion requests, the panel displays a checkmark icon and the message "No pending deletion requests." This is the expected and ideal state.

Important notes

  • Check the Deletions panel at least twice a week — especially scan for red chips (≤3 days) which indicate accounts approaching auto-deletion
  • Your deletion PIN is personal and authorises permanent data deletion — do not share it with anyone, including other admins or Super-Admins
  • If you forget your deletion PIN, ask a Super-Admin to reset it — they can set a new one for you without seeing the old one
  • All Delete Now actions are logged in the audit log with your admin account name and timestamp
