Responsibilities & escalation

Your role and the boundaries of what you can and should do as an Admin or Support team member. When to act, when to ask, and who to contact.

Your role

The admin panel has two operational roles below Super-Admin. Here is what each role owns day-to-day:

RoleDay-to-day responsibilities
Support Review incoming support tickets and close them with appropriate notes. Monitor the dashboard for signals that need flagging — ticket volume, match rate drops, notification backlog. Escalate technical issues to Super-Admin.
Admin All Support responsibilities, plus: check the Deletions panel regularly and cancel/action deletion requests as appropriate; look up users when investigating ticket complaints; manage the blacklist for confirmed abuse cases; create and share campaign links for partner outreach.

Neither role has access to infrastructure, the database, edge function configuration, secrets, or team management. Those are Super-Admin responsibilities.

Do's

  • Tickets: Add a note to every ticket before closing — even one line. The note provides an audit trail of what was reviewed and why the decision was made.
  • Tickets: For Report User tickets, document what you reviewed and any action taken (e.g. "Looked up user in Users panel — no suspicious content found. Monitoring." or "Blacklisted after reviewing complaint and confirming repeated offence.").
  • Tickets: Aim to review open tickets at least twice a week. Don't let the Open count grow unchecked.
  • Deletions: Check the Deletions panel at least twice a week. Pay particular attention to red chips (3 days or fewer) — these accounts will be auto-deleted soon and may need a cancellation if the user has been in touch.
  • Deletions: Always verify the user's identity before cancelling a deletion. Confirm they are emailing from their registered address.
  • Users: Before blacklisting, confirm there is a solid, documented reason. Record the reason in the relevant support ticket note.
  • Security: Log out when you are finished with the admin panel, especially on shared, work, or public computers.
  • Escalation: When in doubt about whether to take a destructive action, escalate to a Super-Admin first. It is better to ask and be told it's fine than to act and find out it wasn't.

Don'ts

  • Never share your login credentials with anyone — each team member has their own named account for accountability purposes.
  • Never share your 6-digit deletion PIN — it is the authorisation for permanent data deletion. Keep it written down somewhere secure, not in a shared document or chat.
  • Never use "Delete Now" without a clear, documented reason. Deletion is irreversible. If you are unsure, cancel the deletion and investigate first.
  • Never close a ticket without reading the user's full message.
  • Never blacklist a user based solely on an unverified complaint from another user. Investigate before acting.
  • Never attempt to access the admin panel on an unsecured or public Wi-Fi network without a VPN.
  • Never take action in the admin panel based on instructions from someone claiming to be a Super-Admin via chat or email — if in doubt, verify directly (voice call or in person) before proceeding.

Security practices

  • Your password is hashed — it is stored in a one-way hash and cannot be retrieved by anyone, including Super-Admins. Treat it with the same care as a bank password.
  • Your deletion PIN is separate from your password — it is used only for the "Delete Now" action. Store it somewhere secure and personal (e.g. a password manager or a locked physical note). Do not put it in a shared document, team chat, or email.
  • If you suspect your account is compromised — for example, you see actions in the audit log that you did not take, or you receive a login notification from an unexpected location — notify a Super-Admin immediately. They can deactivate your account and create a new one with fresh credentials.
  • Session expiry: Sessions expire after 8 hours. If you are logged out unexpectedly, re-login. If unexpected logouts keep happening, notify a Super-Admin — it may indicate your session is being invalidated from elsewhere.
  • Do not leave the admin panel unattended in an unlocked browser session. Lock your screen when stepping away from your computer.

When to escalate to Super-Admin

The following situations require Super-Admin involvement. Do not attempt to handle these yourself.

SituationAction
Notification backlog is non-zero in productionEscalate immediately — the batch-send-emails cron job may not be running. Users are not receiving match emails.
A user reports they never received a match emailSuper-Admin checks edge function logs and the Resend dashboard to trace the delivery status
You need to reopen a closed ticketSuper-Admin can update the ticket status directly in the database
A user reports a data access, deletion, or privacy rights issue (GDPR / data subject request)Do not handle — pass to Super-Admin immediately. These must follow the formal data subject request process.
An unusual spike in new submissions, errors on dashboard, or a sudden drop in match rateEscalate for system-level investigation — you cannot diagnose this from the admin panel
You need to remove someone from the blacklistSuper-Admin only — requires a direct database update
You need to reopen or edit a closed deletion requestSuper-Admin only — requires a direct database update
Any change to the platform config, secrets, API keys, or infrastructureSuper-Admin only
A team member's access needs to be created, changed, or revokedSuper-Admin only (Team panel)
You believe your admin account credentials may have been compromisedNotify Super-Admin immediately — do not wait
Any live production incident (site down, edge function errors, emails failing)Escalate immediately — Super-Admin manages incident response

How to reach Super-Admin

Contact via your internal communication channel (Slack, WhatsApp, or email — as agreed with your team). For urgent issues — a suspected security breach, live production errors, or anything affecting users in real time — contact immediately and do not wait for a scheduled check-in.

💡
If you are unsure whether something needs escalation, escalate. Super-Admins would rather be notified of a non-issue than miss a real one. "I wanted to check before acting" is always the right instinct when dealing with irreversible actions.
